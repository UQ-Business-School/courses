Three Differentiation Levers

Beyond SOLO's structural complexity: three independent ways to challenge students across the same intellectual terrain — Scope, Complexity, and Autonomy.

⏱ ~15 minutes
The Framework

Three Levers You Can Pull

SOLO describes how students structure their thinking. But it doesn't prescribe what to ask them to think about or how much freedom to give them. The Three Differentiation Levers fill that gap. Each lever can be pulled independently: you can increase scope without increasing complexity, or demand autonomy while keeping scope narrow.

Scope

How wide did they look?

Narrow: Single source, single perspective, single case.
Moderate: Multiple sources or perspectives; comparative analysis.
Broad: Cross-disciplinary, macro-level, systemic view.

Complexity

How deeply did they think?

Simple/surface: Apply a single concept; identify factors independently.
Moderate connections: See how two or three ideas interact; explain tensions.
Complex interactions: Synthesise multiple perspectives; handle contradictions and emergent properties.

Autonomy

How independently did they work?

Guided/prompted: Follow provided structure; apply given framework.
Structured independently: Design own structure within clear parameters; justify choices.
Self-directed and original: Choose what to study, how to approach it, which tools to use.

Key insight: These three levers are independent. A descriptor can pull high on Scope while staying modest on Complexity (e.g., "synthesises 5+ sources" without requiring interactions between them). A single criterion can pull all three, or focus on just one.
