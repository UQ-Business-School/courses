Part A · Foundations — Module 3

Observable Language

The foundational skill of rubric design: describing what students do rather than how good their work is.

⏱ ~20 minutes
Activity 1 of 4

The Transform Challenge

Here are five adverb-heavy descriptors. For each, rewrite it using observable language — describe what the student does, not how well they do it. Then reveal the model answer and rate how close you got.

1
Rewrite Five Descriptors
Original (adverb-scaled) "Demonstrates excellent critical analysis of the case study."
Model Answer Identifies the root causes of the organisational problem (not just symptoms), supports each cause with at least two relevant theories, and explains how the causes interact to produce the observed outcomes.
How close?
Original (adverb-scaled) "Very good use of academic sources."
Model Answer Integrates sources from multiple perspectives, including at least one that challenges the main argument; sources are synthesised (not just summarised) to build a coherent evidence base.
How close?
Original (adverb-scaled) "Provides satisfactory recommendations."
Model Answer Proposes at least two recommendations that are connected to the analysis; recommendations are stated but not yet evaluated for feasibility or connected to broader implications.
How close?
Original (adverb-scaled) "Shows a good understanding of organisational behaviour theories."
Model Answer Accurately defines and applies at least two OB theories to the case, explaining how each theory illuminates a different aspect of the turnover problem.
How close?
Original (adverb-scaled) "Adequate referencing throughout the work."
Model Answer All claims are attributed to sources using APA 7 format; in-text citations match the reference list; minor formatting inconsistencies may be present but do not impede source identification.
How close?

Key insight: There's no single right answer. But notice the patterns in the model answers: they use verbs (identifies, supports, integrates, proposes, applies, defines), they specify quantities or conditions where useful, and they describe things a marker can actually check in the student's work.

Apply It: Rewrite the HD Descriptor

Time to work on the actual MGTS2501 rubric. Take the "Analysis" criterion at HD level and rewrite it from adverb-scaled to observable.

4
Your First Rubric Edit
Current HD descriptor — Analysis (40%) "Demonstrates excellent critical analysis"

Rewrite this descriptor so it describes what a HD-level student actually does in their case study analysis. Think about: What observable actions show HD-level critical analysis of a staff turnover case study?

Model Answer Identifies both immediate and systemic causes of the turnover problem, distinguishing symptoms from root issues. Supports each cause with multiple relevant theories, explaining how they interact. Evaluates management's response against what the theories predict, identifying what was missed. Analysis moves beyond description to build an original, evidence-based interpretation of the case.
How close?
