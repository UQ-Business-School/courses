Course Level Calibration

The same criterion means different things at first-year, second-year, and postgraduate level. Learn to calibrate expectations by course level.

⏱ ~15 minutes
Calibration & Context

Same Criterion, Different Standards

A first-year student who identifies two factors and applies one theory might deserve a Pass (P). A second-year student doing the same thing deserves a Credit (C). A postgraduate student doing the same thing deserves a Fail (F). The criterion is the same; the level of work expected is different.

First-year: Recognise and apply foundational knowledge. Show you understand the basics from course material.

Second-year: Synthesise and evaluate. Integrate multiple sources and perspectives, beginning independent thinking.

Postgraduate: Lead and innovate. Critically synthesise across traditions, challenge orthodoxies, propose original solutions.
