⏱ ~20 minutes
The Framework

Three Frameworks, One Descriptor

Across Part B, you've learned three powerful design tools: Values of Inquiry (what knowledge matters), SOLO (how deeply students understand), and the Three Levers (how to scale challenge). Now you'll apply all three simultaneously. They work in concert: VoI tells you what to assess, SOLO tells you the depth, and Levers tell you how to differentiate between grade levels.

The integration pattern:

VoI values (Clarity, Accuracy, Precision, Breadth, Depth, Relevance, Significance, Coherence, Cogency) define the content. SOLO levels (Unistructural through Extended Abstract) define cognitive progression. The Three Levers (Scope, Complexity, Autonomy) define which dimension you scale to differentiate between grade levels.

Model Descriptors

HD: Proposes recommendations that address root causes, with detailed implementation plans, anticipated barriers, and evaluation criteria. Recommendations are justified with evidence and demonstrate transferable principles applicable beyond this specific case.

D: Proposes recommendations that address both symptoms and root causes, with implementation steps and consideration of feasibility. Each recommendation is explicitly linked to the analysis.

C: Proposes recommendations connected to the identified problems. Implementation is mentioned but not detailed. Recommendations are reasonable but not prioritised or evaluated.

P: Proposes at least two recommendations relevant to the case. Recommendations are stated but not clearly linked to the analysis or evaluated for feasibility.

MF: Proposes a recommendation that has some connection to the case but is vague or generic, without clear links to the analysis.

F: Recommendations are absent or entirely disconnected from the analysis presented.

LF: No recommendations provided, or recommendations demonstrate fundamental misunderstanding of the case.
