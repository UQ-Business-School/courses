Part C · Calibration & Context — Module 14

CALD Accessibility

Culturally and Linguistically Diverse (CALD) students navigate rubrics written in idiom, jargon, and cultural assumption. Rewrite your criteria for clarity: literal, specific, and globally fair.

⏱ ~15 minutes
The Issue

Language Barriers in Rubrics

A rubric phrase like "thinks outside the box" is opaque to non-native speakers and culturally specific. It says nothing about the actual capability. When you rewrite for clarity, you don't dumb down—you clarify. Everyone benefits.

The accessibility principle:

Clear rubrics help all learners. When you replace idiom with specificity, when you separate language quality from content, when you name cultural assumptions and reject them, you create rubrics that are fair, honest, and universally understood.

Delivery Criteria Problem

A presentation rubric says: "Maintains confident eye contact", "Shows assertive body language", "Commands the room with vocal presence." These are culturally specific and inaccessible. Identify them and rewrite to focus on communication strategy, not style.

Click on a criterion to highlight it as culturally specific and rewrite it.

Model Revision

Revised criterion: "Maintains audience engagement through visual, verbal, or physical communication strategies appropriate to the context." This focuses on outcome (engagement) and allows for cultural variation in how that's achieved. It removes assumptions about what "assertive" or "confident" looks like.
