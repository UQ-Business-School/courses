    The Coordinator Test: Text Transform

    Write a brief rationale for one criterion change—explaining to the course coordinator why a specific change was made and why it matters. Example: "The original 'Introduction (10%)' became 'Problem Identification (15%)' and here's why: The original criterion assessed a section of the assignment rather than a transferable skill. 'Problem Identification' assesses the student's ability to recognise and articulate the core issue, which is assessable across different assignment formats and more useful as feedback..."

    MGTS2501 · Assessment A2: Case Study Analysis

    Criterion High Distinction (85–100%)
    Problem Identification
    10%    		 Identifies both immediate and systemic causes of the turnover problem, distinguishing symptoms from root issues. Diagnosis is specific to the case context.
    Critical Analysis*
    35% (GA2)    		 Supports each cause with multiple relevant theories, explaining how they interact. Evaluates management's response against what the theories predict, identifying assumptions, biases, and gaps in the analysis. Analysis moves beyond description to build an original, evidence-based interpretation.
    Evidence Integration
    25%    		 Selects evidence from case data and scholarly sources that directly supports each theoretical argument. Explains the relevance of evidence to the specific context.
    Structure & Clarity
    20%    		 Argument is logically sequenced. Transitions between ideas are explicit. Writing is clear, concise, and maintains professional register throughout.
    Reflection & Limitations
    10%    		 Acknowledges limitations in the analysis or the case data. Considers alternative interpretations. Reflects on what the analysis reveals about management practice more broadly.

    * Denotes criterion mapped to Graduate Attribute GA2: Courageous Thinkers (Critical Analysis)

    Sub-criteria assessed: Critical Analysis, Evidence Synthesis, Originality
