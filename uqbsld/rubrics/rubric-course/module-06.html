SOLO Taxonomy

Structural complexity as grade progression — a framework that describes how student responses become more sophisticated, not just "better."

⏱ ~20 minutes
The Framework

Structure, Not Quality

SOLO (Structure of the Observed Learning Outcome) describes five levels of structural complexity. The key insight: higher grades don't mean "better" in some vague way — they mean structurally more complex. Ideas are connected, patterns emerge, principles are abstracted.

1

Pre-structural

Misses the point. Irrelevant, restates the question, or tautological.

2

Uni-structural

One relevant idea in isolation. No connections to other concepts.

3

Multi-structural

Several relevant ideas, but treated independently. A list, not an argument.

4

Relational

Ideas connected. Patterns identified, relationships explained, forming a coherent whole.

5

Extended Abstract

Principles abstracted beyond the case. Applied to new contexts; limitations critiqued.

The key difference from VoI: VoI describes what intellectual qualities are present. SOLO describes how ideas are structured and connected. Both are needed — a response can be coherent (VoI) but only multi-structural (SOLO), or relational (SOLO) but lacking accuracy (VoI).
