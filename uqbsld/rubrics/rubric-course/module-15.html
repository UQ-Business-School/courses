Embedding Requirements

Assessment briefs specify requirements (word counts, source minimums, formatting standards). Rubrics often ignore them. This module shows how to embed them explicitly into grade levels.

⏱ ~15 minutes

The Count Metrics Trap

Numbers trap us. It's tempting to use counts as quality proxies, but counts measure requirements, not quality. This quiz tests the difference.

3
Which Is the Quality Proxy?

Read both descriptors. Which one actually measures quality of analysis?

Descriptor A: "Identifies 4 or more contributing factors"

Descriptor B: "Identifies contributing factors and explains how they interact"

A — "4 or more factors" is the better quality proxy
B — "Explains interactions" is the better quality proxy

Embed Requirements into Your Rubric

4
Write Evidence Integration

Use these model answers as a guide, then write your own for Pass and Credit levels.

Model: Pass Level

"Meets the minimum requirement of 8 peer-reviewed sources. References formatted in APA 7 with some errors. Submission within 10% of 2,000-word limit."

Model: Credit Level

"Meets or exceeds 8-source minimum with appropriate range of seminal and recent research. APA 7 formatting with only minor inconsistencies. Submission meets 2,000-word requirement."
