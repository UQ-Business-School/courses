GA & AoL Explained

Integrating Graduate Attributes and Assurance of Learning into Assessment Rubrics

Overview

What We're Doing

The Business School is integrating UQ's six Graduate Attributes (GAs) into assessment rubrics across all courses. Each course is being mapped to two GAs, and the criteria for those GAs are being woven into the marking rubrics for specific assessments.

This serves two purposes. First, it strengthens the quality of our assessment by ensuring rubrics reflect program-level capabilities, not just task-specific requirements. Second, it enables the school to meet AACSB Assurance of Learning (AoL) requirements for accreditation by embedding measurable criteria directly into the rubrics markers already use — no separate data collection needed.

The Six GAs

The Six Graduate Attributes

Each GA has a standardised set of three assessment criteria developed at the Business School level. These criteria define what the GA looks like in practice when assessed through student work.

Accomplished Scholars

  • Contribution to Field
GA2

GA6
Respectful Leaders
  • Application of Ethics
  • Relational Leadership
  • Responsible Decision-Making
The Process

How It Works in Practice

Each course is mapped to two GAs, each tied to a specific assessment worth at least 10% of the course grade. The GA criteria are then integrated directly into the assessment's marking rubric — not bolted on as separate rows, but woven into existing criteria so the rubric remains a coherent marking tool.

The Learning Design team drafts the revised rubrics. Course coordinators review and approve. The goal is to preserve the coordinator's pedagogical intent while ensuring the GA criteria are identifiable within the rubric.

Integration Tiers

Two Levels of Integration

General Curriculum Uplift
Applies To
All courses in mapped majors
Criteria
Minimum 2 of 3 GA criteria integrated into the rubric. All 3 if they align naturally.
Purpose
Ensures every course contributes to GA development and can be drawn on for accreditation evidence if needed.
AACSB Assurance of Learning
Applies To
Courses nominated for AoL reporting (e.g., program core courses)
Criteria
All 3 of 3 GA criteria must be integrated and identifiable, so accreditation reviewers can map rubric scores directly to GA outcomes.
Purpose
Provides the direct evidence AACSB requires — markers' rubric scores become the AoL data, with no additional assessment burden.

The general uplift means the school builds a broad base of GA-integrated assessment across all its courses. The AoL tier then targets specific courses — typically program core — where the accreditation team needs to collect and report on student achievement against each GA criterion.

For Coordinators

What This Means for Course Coordinators

For most coordinators, the process looks like this:

1
The Learning Design team identifies which two GAs map to your course and which assessments are best suited for integration.
2
We draft a revised rubric that integrates the GA criteria into your existing marking criteria.
3
You review the draft and let us know if anything doesn't align with how you teach or assess.
4
Once approved, we handle the admin — updating JAC profiles, course change forms, and ECP records.
Key Principles: Authentic alignment only. We only map GAs where the assessment genuinely develops that capability.
